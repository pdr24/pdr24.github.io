About Me

I’m an undergraduate Honors student at the University of Texas at San Antonio, majoring in Computer Science with concentrations in Data Science and Software Engineering and a minor in Mathematics. My academic and research interests center on computer vision, distributed deep learning, and developing software tools for K-12 AI education.

I have contributed to research in multiple research labs at the University of Texas at San Antonio: Dr. Amanda Fernandez’s Vision and AI Lab, Dr. Palden Lama’s Cloud Systems (CloudSys Lab), Dr. Fred Martin’s Engaging Computing Group, and Dr. Rajendra Boppana’s Systems and Networks (SyN) Lab. I have worked on research projects involving multimodal defect detection for nuclear materials applications, distributed deep learning for resource-constrained edge devices, software tools for supporting K-12 AI literacy, and machine learning driven malware detection. My work has led to peer-reviewed publications, poster presentations, and competitive undergraduate research awards.

My long-term goal is to develop efficient, explainable, and accessible AI systems. I intend to pursue a Ph.D. in Computer Science to expand my research in explainable deep learning and machine learning, as well as their real-world applications.
